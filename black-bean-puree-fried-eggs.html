Black Bean Purée & Fried Eggs with Avocado & Grape Tomatoes

High protein and high fiber for satiety. Black beans and flaxseed support soluble fiber intake.

Estimated nutrition

~620 kcalCalories
~31–34 gProtein
~46 gCarbs
~20–22 gFiber
~32 gFat

Grab ingredients

  • 75 g avocado
  • 150 g cooked black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 80 ml low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 3 large eggs
  • 75 g grape tomatoes
  • 20 g jalapeño pepper, optional
  • 60 g yellow onion
  • 10 g ground flaxseed
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil, for bean purée
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil, for eggs
  • ¼ tsp chili powder
  • ¼ tsp ground cumin
  • ⅛ tsp salt
  • ⅛ tsp black pepper

Cook & enjoy

  1. Wash and dry the grape tomatoes, avocado, and jalapeño.
  2. Preheat a small saucepan over medium heat. Meanwhile, finely dice the yellow onion.
  3. Add 1 tsp olive oil to the saucepan. Cook the onion until softened, about 3–4 minutes. Add the diced jalapeño and cook for another 30 seconds.
  4. Drain and rinse the black beans. Add them to the saucepan with broth, chili powder, cumin, salt, and black pepper. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  5. Mash the beans using a potato masher or immersion blender until mostly smooth. Stir in ground flaxseed and cook another minute until slightly thickened. Remove from heat and cover.
  6. Heat a nonstick skillet over medium heat. Add 1 tsp olive oil and cook the eggs until the whites are set and the yolks remain runny, about 2–3 minutes.
  7. Halve the grape tomatoes and dice the avocado. Mix together in a bowl and season lightly if desired.
  8. Spoon the black bean purée into a bowl. Top with fried eggs and serve the avocado-tomato mixture on the side.

Why this works

  • High protein and high fiber for satiety.
  • Black beans and flaxseed support soluble fiber intake.
  • Eggs provide choline and complete protein.
  • Avocado adds potassium and monounsaturated fats.
  • Good fit for the first week after your period when rebuilding structure matters.
